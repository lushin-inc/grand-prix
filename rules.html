How the bracket gets set

Your four pool games decide who you play at 12:30 Sunday — or whether you play at all.

3points — win
1point — tie
0points — loss

Three or more teams tied? Head-to-head is skipped — it starts at goal differential. Note the cap: goal differential only counts up to 7 per game, so running up the score past 7 earns nothing.

Game clock

Halves
Two 20-minute halves, running clock.
Pool play
No overtime. A tie stays a tie and both teams take a point.
Playoff
4-minute sudden victory. Still level after that → 3v3 Braveheart.
Championship
Unlimited 4-minute sudden-victory periods, one timeout each per period.

Timeouts

Each game
One 30-second timeout.
Pool play catch
If you’re ahead, you can’t call it in the last 2 minutes.
Playoff & final
Clock stops in the second half — either team can use it.

Counts

Clear the midline
20 seconds. Once the count expires you have to stay in the offensive half — over-and-back is on.
2034 and younger
No counts at all, except the goalie’s 4-second. Doesn’t apply to 2033.

Mercy rule

Pool play only
Down by 7 or more, the trailing team can elect to take the ball at midfield after a goal instead of facing off.
Back to normal
Once the lead is 6 or fewer, standard faceoffs resume.

Penalties

Technical
30 seconds, or possession awarded.
Personal
1–3 minutes, releasable or non-releasable.
Clock
The penalty clock stops for injuries, timeouts, end of periods, and any new penalty.
Fighting
Ejected and suspended the next game — and that includes leaving the sideline or crossing midfield to join in. Multiple offenders can forfeit the game and get the team disqualified.

Weather

Lightning
Fields clear for 30 minutes after the last strike. Five horns means play resumes.
Heat
Game times can shift based on safety readings (wet bulb globe temperature).
Anything else
Directors can move games for field conditions, visibility, or temperature.

Before he plays

Waiver
Online player waiver has to be done before he steps on the field.
Equipment
Legal and uniformed per USA Lacrosse / NFHS.
Mouthguard
Mandatory. No exceptions.

Roster & conduct

One team
No playing for two teams in the same age group, or for different clubs. Violations mean game disqualification and adjusted scores.
Disputes
Raise them immediately — once scores are finalized it’s over.
Zero tolerance
Alcohol, drugs, discrimination, or poor behavior from players, coaches or fans. Refs and the director can stop play for any reason.
